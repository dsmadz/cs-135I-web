Shotgun Willie Chili

I don't know when we first started making this chili, I think late 90's, but it is the most flavorful chili we have made. We found the recipe on "food network" and gave it a try. The rest is history.

The recipe was originally created by Carol & Dave Hancock. Here is a link to the story behind them and their original recipe at chilicookoff.com.

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs chuck, cubed or coarse ground
  • 6 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 6 dried New Mexico chiles
  • 6 dried pasilla chiles
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon oregano leaves
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2 medium onions or 1 large, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon hot red pepper sauce
  • 8 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon monosodium glutamate (msg)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1

Place 1 cup of water in a small saucepan and add the oregano. Bring to a boil, remove from the heat and steep like tea. Strain, and set liquid aside.

Step 2

Remove stems and seeds from pepper pods and boil chile peppers in water for approximately 1/2 hour. Let them cool, then add the peppers and the oregano liquid to a blender. Puree until smooth, adding some of the water from the boiled peppers if necessary. Strain through a fine mesh sieve. I use at least 2 cups of this puree in the recipe. The rest freezes nicely.

Step 3

In a large pot, brown the beef in hot oil, then add the onions cooking until translucent.

Step 4

Add the pepper puree and remaining ingredients stirring well. Cover and simmer for 2-3 hours, until meat is tender, stirring occasionally.

Step 5

Serve and garnish with cilantro, sour cream, cheese or any other toppings that you like.
